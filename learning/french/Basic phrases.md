Je - I
Tu - You
Nous - We
Elle/il - her/him
Elles/ils - they/they

When referring to a group of exclusively females, you use elles, when referring to a group of exclusively males or a mixed group, you use ils.

---

The ending of a verb depends on the pronoun.

**Pronoun** is the word to whom a verb is referring to, such as I, you, him, her, we, they etc.

| Pronoun   | Verb                          |
| --------- | ----------------------------- |
| Je        | travaille                     |
| Tu        | travailles (ends with -es/-s) |
| Elle/Il   | travaille                     |
| Nous      | travaillons (ends with -ons)  |
| Elles/Ils | travaillent (ends with -ent)  |

Depending on whether an adjective is used on a feminine or a masculine noun, its ending may change. Usually masculine adjectives stay as they are, whereas feminine adjectives get an additional -e at the end.

Il est français (masculine)
Elle est française (feminine)

Tu es anglais (masculine)
Tu es anglaise (feminine)

Je suis américain (masculine)
Je suis américaine (feminine)
